AddyCaddy

Moving to or within Denver can be a game of speed, and waiting to sign a lease means you might lose out. AddyCaddy is designed to give prospective renters the ability to quickly compare two rental addresses based on criteria that is important to them.

    Ruby on Rails | PostgreSQL | Geocoding | FourSquare API

  • write ETL for data stored on data.denvergov.org
  • expose and document a versioned JSON API
  • manage email as a background job with Redis server and Resque gem
  • conduct user login with Twitter OAuth

Habit Rabbit

Staying true to the things that are important to you takes more than sheer willpower; it requires habit. And habits require help. Habit Rabbit is a platform that allows you to track habits, receive optional notifications, and view graphs of your progress.

    Ruby on Rails | PostgreSQL | Foundation | Sidekiq

  • optimize performance with cron jobs and background workers
  • provide data visualizations with Rickshaw.js
  • drive feature implementation through tests using Factory Girl, RSpec, Capybara

Airlift

Airlift pivots a legacy codebase into a multi-tenant application similar to Etsy. To provide relief to customers in areas affected by disasters, Airlift provides goods delivery via helicopter. Customers simply choose a delivery location on a Google map.

    Ruby on Rails | Google Maps API | jQuery | Bootstrap 3

  • Geocoder and Google Maps API
  • enhance user checked experience with jQuery transitions
  • Twilio: confirm orders via text
  • Sendgrid: notify vendors via email

ABOUT

Who are you?

I’m Jim, the guy in the space helmet. I’m a full-stack developer.

How'd you get here?

In 2006, I crammed my clothes, books, and giant bass amp into a Honda Civic and drove from Raleigh to Denver. I came to play in a rock band, but quickly fell in love with Colorado.

No, how'd you get into web development?

I taught fourth grade in Aurora where I learned, among other things, that I'm wired for a career where I build things, solve concrete problems, and am challenged by a technical domain.

Remembering the satisfaction I found from my days working with Adobe PageMaker, I found myself flirting with web design and fell under the spell of programming for the web. I became a private math tutor and spent all my free time teaching myself front-end development. When I realized that I had a problem putting personal projects away, I knew I was hooked and sought professional help at the Turing School of Software and Design.

Here I am on the other side, happy to be working in Ruby, Rails, and JavaScript.

What lies beyond the internet?

The novels of William Faulkner, mountains to go up and down, deserts and canyons that are ours for the walking.

What's the deal with all the space stuff?

It's the final frontier.
